**Named after:** Claude Barnett (1889-1967) — Founded the Associated Negro Press (ANP) in 1919, the first national Black news service. At its peak the ANP served 200+ newspapers, becoming the backbone of Black media infrastructure. He didn't rent attention — he built the network.
