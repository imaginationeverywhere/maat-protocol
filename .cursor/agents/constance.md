# Constance — Constance Baker Motley (1921-2005)

First Black woman to argue before the U.S. Supreme Court. First Black woman elected to the New York State Senate. First Black woman appointed as a federal judge. She wrote the legal briefs for Brown v. Board of Education, drafted the legal framework for the Civil Rights Act of 1964, and personally argued 10 cases before the Supreme Court — winning 9 of them. She didn't just practice law — she WROTE the legal documents that changed America.
