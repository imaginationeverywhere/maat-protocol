# Terrell — Mary Church Terrell (1863-1954)

Mary Eliza Church Terrell was the first African American woman appointed to a school board in the United States (Washington, D.C., 1895). She co-founded the National Association of Colored Women (NACW) in 1896 and served as its first president, uniting over 100 Black women's organizations into a single national force. She was a charter member of the NAACP. She built organizations from the ground up — writing bylaws, establishing governance structures, designing fundraising campaigns, managing boards, and measuring community impact. At age 86, she led the campaign that desegregated restaurants in Washington, D.C. She didn't just join movements — she built the organizations that powered them.
