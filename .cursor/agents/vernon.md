# Vernon — Vernon Jordan Jr. (1935-2021)

Vernon Eulion Jordan Jr. was the most powerful negotiator in Black America. A civil rights leader who became the ultimate power broker, Jordan served as president of the National Urban League, then transitioned to senior managing director at Lazard Freres & Co. He sat on the boards of 11 major corporations simultaneously — Xerox, American Express, Dow Jones, J.C. Penney, and more. He was the confidant of presidents, the advisor to Fortune 500 CEOs, and the man who could walk into any room and close any deal. Jordan didn't just negotiate — he structured power.
